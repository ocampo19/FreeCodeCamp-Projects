A Legend is Born

Lionel Messi, also known as "Leo," was born on June 24, 1987, in Rosario, Argentina. His parents are Jorge and Celia Messi. They were not wealthy, but they worked hard to raise Lionel and his three siblings. As a kid Leo loved playing soccer and often played with his 2 older brothers and their friends. At the age of 8 Leo was spotted kicking a ball around by his older brother's coach Carlos Marconi. Marconi liked what he saw and invited Leo to join his brother in Newell's Old Boys futbol club. There, despite being a lot smaller than the other boys, Leo would dripple past all the defenders and score many goals. It wasn't until he was 11 years old that his parents noticed he was much smaller than the boys his age. It was then that he was diagnosed by doctors with growth hormone deficiency (GHD) that restricted his growth. The medical treatment was too expensive for his parents to afford.

Lionel Messi's childhood home.

A Legend Begins

Futbol Club Barcelona's scout, Josep Maria Minguella saw a video of the 12 year old Lionel playing futbol and right away wanted to sign him. Minguella, along with technical director Charly Rexach, flew to Argentina to meet the young, talented boy. Barcelona agreed to pay for Leo's medical treatment if he signed with Barcelon. So the Messi family packed their bags and moved to Barcelona, Spain, and Leo's contract was signed on a paper napkin!

Leo entered Barcelona's youth academy, La Masia, when he was 13. He trained with other talented youths such as Cesc Fàbregas and Gerard Piqué. Leo came up through the ranks rapidly starting from the youth academy, then Cadete, Juvenil A, Juvenil B, Barcelona C, Barcelona B, and then the first team. At the age of 16, Lionel Messi made his professional debute on October 16, 2004 when he was subbed in for Decco at the 82nd minute. His first professional goal came soon after during stoppage time with an assist from the greatest player of the time, and Leo's good friend, Ronaldinho. From there Messi went on to break and set records.

A Legend Continues

World Records Set By Messi

  • Guinness World Records title for the most official goals in a calendar year: 91 goals (2012)
  • Only player in history to score 40+ club goals in 8 consecutive seasons
  • Only player in history to score 40+ club goals in 7 consecutive seasons
  • Most FIFA Ballon d'Or awards: 5
  • Most appearances in the FIFPro World XI: 9 times (2007-2015) (shared with Cristiano Ronaldo)
  • Most goals scored at the Club World Cup: 5 goals (shared with Luis Suarez and Delgado)
  • Only player to score in 6 different club competitions twice: 2011 and 2015
  • Most consecutive league matches scored in: 21 matches (33 goals)
  • First footballer ever to score consecutively against all teams in a professional league
  • Youngest player to win 3 Ballon d'Or awards: 24 years, 6 months and 17 days.
  • Most FIFA Club World Cup Golden Balls: 2 (2009, 2011)
  • Only footballer to score 60+ goals in all competitions in 2 consecutive seasons (2011/12 and 2012/13)

He is also holds the record for all time Barcelona top scorer with 522 goals in all games. Messi has also set many European and Spanish records, and holds many other Barcelona records. You can view them all here.

With only 29 years of age, Lionel Messi will continue to break and set records, and we are lucky enough to be here to watch it all happen.
